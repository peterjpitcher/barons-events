1. Claim 1: **PARTIALLY CORRECT**.  
The nesting is real: outer update forms start at [users-manager.tsx](/Users/peterpitcher/Cursor/BARONS-BaronsHub/src/components/users/users-manager.tsx:178) and [users-manager.tsx](/Users/peterpitcher/Cursor/BARONS-BaronsHub/src/components/users/users-manager.tsx:280); `ResendInviteButton` is inserted inside them at [users-manager.tsx](/Users/peterpitcher/Cursor/BARONS-BaronsHub/src/components/users/users-manager.tsx:210) and [users-manager.tsx](/Users/peterpitcher/Cursor/BARONS-BaronsHub/src/components/users/users-manager.tsx:305); the button itself renders its own `<form>` at [resend-invite-button.tsx](/Users/peterpitcher/Cursor/BARONS-BaronsHub/src/components/users/resend-invite-button.tsx:34). But Claude’s causal jump is too strong. I reproduced a minimal React 19 SSR/hydration case locally: the invalid HTML does cause a hydration mismatch, but React then regenerates two separate forms client-side. So this is a real bug, but not proof that resend “doesn’t work” in hydrated React. Claude also missed that the same nesting can perturb the outer save form and surrounding controls, not just resend.

Example: `Tom & Jerry <Admin>` becomes `Tom &amp;amp; Jerry &amp;lt;Admin&amp;gt;` in HTML. Claude missed that the plaintext part is also wrong, because `intro` is reused unmodified in `textParts` at [notifications.ts](/Users/peterpitcher/Cursor/BARONS-BaronsHub/src/lib/notifications.ts:188). He also missed the inconsistency with the normal helper `buildGreeting`, which keeps names raw and relies on single escaping later at [notifications.ts](/Users/peterpitcher/Cursor/BARONS-BaronsHub/src/lib/notifications.ts:299). This is a rendering bug, not a delivery blocker.
